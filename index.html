What are the symptoms?

These are the symptoms of Coronavirus according to the World Health Organization(WHO) :

  • Respiratory symptoms
  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Breathing difficulties
If you have similar symptoms, alert the authorities using the Viral App and seek medical support as Soon as possible.
Please see the Safety Precautions below in order to keep your loved ones safe.

What Could Happen to You?

In more severe cases, the infection can cause :

  • Pneumonia
  • Severe acute respiratory syndrome
  • Kidney failure
  • Death

Safety Precautions

WHO’s standard recommendations for the general public to reduce exposure to and transmission of a range of illnesses are as follows, which include hand and respiratory hygiene, and safe food practices:

  • - Frequently clean hands by using alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water.
  • - When coughing and sneezing cover mouth and nose with flexed elbow or tissue – Throw tissue away immediately and wash hands
  • - Avoid close contact with anyone who has fever and cough.
  • - If you have fever, cough and difficulty breathing seek medical care early and share previous travel history with your healthcare provider.
  • - When visiting live markets in areas currently experiencing cases of novel coronavirus, avoid direct unprotected contact with live animals and surfaces in contact with animals.
  • - The consumption of raw or undercooked animal products should be avoided. Raw meat, milk or animal organs should be handled with care, to avoid cross-contamination with uncooked foods, as per good food safety practices.

About Us

Why us?

The Coronavirus is spreading faster than we expect. The biggest challenge the world faces is to break the line of transmission of the virus from person to person. Our mission is to use our collective skillset to provide a solution to the World Health Organization to tackle the Coronavirus epidemic

Who are we?

We are a movement that started with two students that wanted to do something to help. We have now brought together a cross-functional volunteer project team with the simple message to create a support app for the coronavirus.

What is Viral?

This team is working to create a web app called Viral- a ping system for the infected is a website designed to help you get immediate medical assistance from the relevant authorities. Use the app to ping authorities if you are having symptoms to make sure the situation of you and those around you does not worsen.
